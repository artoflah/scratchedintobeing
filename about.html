SCRATCHED INTO BEING

2025
Cover detail, acrylic and scaffolding panel.

THE ARCHIVE

This website is a digital archive — a living record of presence. A compendium of desperate marks left by people trying to prove they exist in a city designed to make them disappear. Each scratch, scrawl, and carve is a small rebellion against erasure.

THE BOOK

Scratched Into Being began as a physical object — an acrylic-covered book, bound with metal hinges, its surface salvaged from a "POST NO BILLS" scaffolding panel.

Inside, translucent vellum pages overlap and blur, revealing fragments of names, numbers, and confessions. Clear acetate overlays certain tags, and fold-out pages extend beyond the frame — a layered structure mirroring the way the city itself collects and hides its stories.

THE GESTURE

It documents the small, furious gestures etched into the urban landscape — proof that someone was here, someone mattered, when everything else made them feel like ghosts.

In concrete canyons where anonymity is architecture, these marks become monuments: a name carved into painted metal, initials on a bathroom stall, a phone number scratched into plexiglass, a declaration of love on a park bench.

They whisper: I was here. I mattered. I existed.

THE PROJECT

This digital space continues that conversation. Click and drag on any image to leave your own mark — a digital scratch layered over the archive, merging your existence with those who came before.

Each gesture adds another voice, another proof of life — a collective document of presence in a world built to forget.
